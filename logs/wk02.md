## AI interactions

### Interaction 1

- **What I asked**: "Take my hello.html and add a navigation bar, a section with a list of my interests, and a footer. Use different HTML tags I haven't seen before."
- **What AI gave me**: An expanded version of my hello.html with a `<nav>` containing links, a `<section>` for my interests using `<ul>` and `<li>`, and a `<footer>` at the bottom. It also introduced semantic tags like `<header>`, `<main>`, and `<article>` that I hadn't used before.
- **What I kept / changed / rejected**: Used the READ-FIND-ASK-VERIFY workflow before pasting. Marked the tags I didn't recognize (`<nav>`, `<main>`, `<article>`), asked Claude to explain just those, and then changed one thing on my own (edited the nav links and swapped one tag) to prove I actually understood it.

### Interaction 2
- **What I asked**: I asked Claude to explain the difference between `<section>`, `<article>`, and `<div>` since they all looked like they did the same thing
- **What AI gave me**: `<div>` has no meaning, it's just a generic box. `<section>` is a thematic group of content (like a chapter). `<article>` is a self-contained piece of content that could stand on its own (like a blog post or a news story). Semantic tags help screen readers and search engines understand the page.
- **What I kept / changed / rejected**: Kept the explanation as a mental rule. Went back into my hello.html and replaced a couple of generic `<div>`s with the right semantic tags.
